About

Hello stranger. My name is Lingyu Zhou, aka "Yuin". I am a now graduated student from Cornell University, also the founder of KH Entertainment.

I try to deliver the best UX from data analysis, also explore the numbers and their connections behind, using applied mathematics and computer engineering.

Previously, I studied applied mathematics at University of Wisconsin-Madison for my bachelor's degree. I am now working as a data analyst who works mainly on labor compliance survey data in east Asia for Professor Sarosh Kuruvilla at Cornell IRL. Previously I worked at New York Sea Grant as a data assistant, Jiangsu Hoperun Software Co. Ltd. as a data engineer intern, and Shanghai Sino Environmental Technology Co., Ltd as a data analyst.

My specialties are software engineering, math, data analysis, and data visualization.

I am also interested in etymology, biology, and botany.

Why .NET instead of .COM? The pervasive misappropriation of the ".com" domain for personal websites represents a linguistic and conceptual misunderstanding, indicative of a lexical disregard for the commercial connotation inherent in this TLD. The ubiquitous adoption of ".com" for non-commercial pursuits commit a departure from the etymological underpinnings of the abbreviation, where "com" ostensibly denotes commercial endeavors. Such lexical transgressions, seems subtle, illuminate the nuanced and evolving nature of language in the virtual epoch, where lexical boundaries are often porous and subject to the whims of cultural appropriation. I strongly condemn misbehavior of this kind. Totally not because I didn't manage to snap up the .com website of my name.
